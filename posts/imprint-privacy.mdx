## Preventive healthcare and related services
Data collected from patients, contractual partners, or interested parties is processed under Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR so that we can provide our contractual or pre-contractual services. The type, scope, and purpose of the processed data are determined by the contractual relationship. In principle, this includes:

- name
- address
- email
- phone number
- contract data, such as costs, products, contact persons, and services
- financial data, including bank details

Data covered by Art. 9(1) GDPR, such as health information, requires the patient's explicit consent under Art. 6(1)(a), Art. 7, and Art. 9(2)(a) GDPR.

Data collected for preventive healthcare is processed under Art. 9(2)(h) GDPR and Section 22(1)(1)(b) BDSG. Explicit consent is also required here. Where necessary, collected data may be disclosed or transmitted to third parties within the limits of statutory provisions and for the purpose of fulfilling the contract, for example to further medical treatment providers, health insurers, laboratories, or similar recipients under Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR and Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR. This may happen where legally required, where necessary to protect another natural person, or on the basis of consent under Art. 6(1)(a) and Art. 7 GDPR.

Data is deleted after fulfillment of our contract and statutory duties of care. Warranty obligations and comparable obligations remain unaffected. We review data and deadlines every three years. Statutory retention periods otherwise apply.
